Signature Win

The $35K+ Account: From Cold Email Pitch to Shopify Migration to Retainer

I reached out cold to an Australian business owner who was running her store on a custom platform with a third-party payment gateway. My initial pitch was for email marketing — I had done my research on her setup, identified gaps in her customer engagement, and proposed how we could improve it. She was interested enough to get on a call. We ran a full audit and presented it to her.

Then she went quiet for two and a half months. Instead of chasing, I waited — and timed my re-engagement around a Shopify migration campaign we were running that offered a discount for clients making the switch. I reached out specifically about Shopify, knowing she was on a custom platform that was costing her more to maintain than it should. That email landed at exactly the right moment: she was already planning a migration but had been delaying it for over a year, struggling to find an agency that could move fast enough.

I got back on a call with her alongside my senior manager. We presented a detailed migration proposal — plugins, structural changes, timeline, cost breakdown. Within two to three weeks of negotiation, she agreed. We completed the full Shopify migration, placed her on a retainer, and then pitched additional services. She chose a combined package of Google Ads, email marketing, and Meta Ads at $2,200/month.

Total yearly pipeline from this single account: $35K+. Started as a cold email. Ended as a multi-service retainer client.

My Approach

How I think about
pipeline generation.

Three principles that show up in every outreach motion I run.

01
Signal before sequence
I don't start a sequence until I understand why this person, at this company, right now. Intent signals, recent hires, funding, tech stack changes — these go into the first line, not the third.
02
Channel follows persona
CMOs at enterprise companies live on LinkedIn. SMB founders pick up the phone. I match the channel to the buyer, not the other way around. Video prospecting when text isn't breaking through.
03
Patience is a strategy
My $35K Australian account started as a cold email, went quiet for two and a half months, and re-opened because I timed my follow-up to a Shopify campaign discount at exactly the right moment. I re-engaged with a specific reason, qualified intent on the call, and the account grew from a single migration to a multi-service retainer. Follow-up with a reason beats follow-up with a reminder every time.
